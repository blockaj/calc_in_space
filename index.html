Jetpacks and Air Pressure

Timmy is in a jet pack. He has an equation to calculate the atmospheric pressure up to 80 km off earth. Once Timmy passes the 80km mark, he has no way of determining the pressure around him. The science boys find the derivative of his equation and plug in 80 km to approximate what the pressure will be once he leaves 80 km.

Using calculus, the science boys found the rate at which pressure was decreasing right before Timmy left the 80km mark. They used this to extrapolate what the atmospheric pressure would be just after Timmy left the atmosphere.

P = pressure, h = height
$$ P = 1035(\frac{1}{2})^{\frac{h}{5.8}} \\ \frac{dP}{dh} = 1035 \cdot ln(\frac{1}{2})\cdot \frac{1}{2}^{\frac{h}{5.8}} \\ \frac{dP}{dh}|_{h=80}=-0.05053915072 \frac{\frac{g}{cm^2}}{km} $$

The Science Boys Send A Satellite Into Space

When their funding was cut, NASA was midway through a project to send a satellite, The Blockey Satellite, into space. The distance from the Earth to this satellite has already been determined. However, for this satellite, the speed must be pretty consistent. If the satellite moves closer or farther away from Earth, the speed will be affected.

Therefore, the science boys will find the derivative of the speed with respect to distance from the Earth at the distance from the Earth the satellite will be and then determine if the rate of change is too great. If so, the satellites distance from Earth will need to be adjusted.

Speed of a satellite = $\sqrt{\frac{G \cdot M_{central}}{R}}$, G = $6.67408 \cdot 10^{-11} \frac{m^3}{kg \cdot s^2}$ (gravitational constant), $M_{central}$ = $5.972 \cdot 10^{24}$ (mass of Earth), R = radius from center of Earth to satellite $$ G \cdot M_{central}=3.9857606 \cdot 10^{14} \\ v = \sqrt{\frac{3.9857606 \cdot 10^{14}}{R}} = (\frac{3.9857606 \cdot 10^{14}}{R})^{\frac{1}{2}} $$ \begin{equation} \begin{split} & f(x) = x^{\frac{1}{2}} \quad & g(R) = \frac{3.9857606 \cdot 10^{14}}{R} \\ & f^\prime(x) = \frac{1}{2}x^{-\frac{1}{2}} \quad & g^\prime(R) = \frac{3.9857606 \cdot 10^{14}}{R^2} \\ \end{split} \end{equation} $$ $$ \begin{equation} \begin{split} \frac{dv}{dR} & = f^\prime(g(R)) \cdot g^\prime(R) \\ \frac{dv}{dR} & = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{3.9857606 \cdot 10^{14}}{R})^{-\frac{1}{2}} \cdot \frac{3.9857606 \cdot 10^{14}}{R^2} \end{split} \end{equation} R = radius of Earth + distance from Earth to satellite = 6371 + 900 = 7271
$\frac{dv}{dR}|_{R=42371}=16.10032\frac{m/s}{km}$
